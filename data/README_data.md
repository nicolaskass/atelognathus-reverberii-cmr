# Data — *Atelognathus reverberii* capture–mark–recapture, Laguna Azul 2019–2020

## `capture_history.csv`

Capture-history matrix for the Pollock's Robust Design analysis.

**Dimensions.** 275 rows × 15 columns:
- 1 ID column (first column): unique individual identifier (VIE colour-code label).
- 14 occasion columns: binary detection (`1` = captured, `0` = not captured) for each of the 14 secondary occasions across three primary sessions.

**Occasion order (columns 2–15).**

| Primary session | Secondary occasions (dates 2019–2020) |
|---|---|
| October 2019 (austral spring, J=4) | 08 Oct, 09 Oct, 10 Oct, 11 Oct |
| November 2019 (J=5) | 24 Nov, 26 Nov, 27 Nov, 28 Nov, 29 Nov |
| February 2020 (late austral summer, J=5) | 01 Feb, 02 Feb, 03 Feb, 04 Feb, 05 Feb |

**Notes.**
- 25 November 2019 was not sampled (adverse weather); that day is not represented as a column.
- 275 individuals were captured at least once across the study. Six distinct history patterns are observed: (1,0,0) = 54; (0,1,0) = 124; (0,0,1) = 73; (1,1,0) = 10; (0,1,1) = 10; (1,0,1) = 4. No individual was detected in all three primary sessions.
- A total of 339 capture events were recorded across all 14 occasions; 40 were within-session recaptures.

## Study site

Laguna Azul, Meseta de Somuncurá, Río Negro Province, Argentina (40°59′S; ~1,050 m a.s.l.). Endorheic shallow clay lagoon, ~2.5 ha, embedded in a basaltic plateau depression.
